### 2026-06-16 — first adaptive session (processed feedback/2026-06-15-gkgr1x.json)
**Signal:** note said *"Scales were good chords were too easy."* Ratings agreed precisely —
C/G and both minor scales all 5; black-key scales (B♭/E♭/G♭, every hand) uniformly 3; every
chord and progression a 5.

**What I changed and why:**
- **Warm-ups trimmed** (C/G to 3 reps) — owned material shouldn't eat the session.
- **Black keys made the focus.** They're the only thing at the edge. Pushed B♭/E♭/G♭ to
  hands-together (5 reps, tempo 84) since that's where coordination is built, kept B♭/E♭
  left-hand-alone (the weak link in HT), and added **A♭ major RH** as the next black-key
  scale while the appetite is there ("scales were good").
- **Minors advanced** — A minor to hands-together; introduced **A harmonic minor** for a new
  ear/finger challenge (the F→G♯ leap) rather than more of what's already easy.
- **Chords advanced decisively** (the explicit "too easy"): block triads → **seventh chords**
  (Dm7–G7–Cmaj7), **two-hand inversions**, and a **C arpeggio** (chord-as-line).
- **Progressions advanced** — the four-chord loop into **G major** (transpose by feel) and a
  **hands-together** version that's a real accompaniment.

Held continuity on the scale material so the black-key progress stays legible. ~29 min,
15 exercises, validated clean.

**Watching for next:** does HT on the black keys drop them below 3 (too big a jump → back off
tempo / return to hands-separate)? Do the sevenths land as a real challenge (3–4) or still
too easy (→ push to four-note voicings in both hands / new progressions)? Is A harmonic
minor's raised 7th awkward (→ isolate the F→G♯ as a two-note drill)?
