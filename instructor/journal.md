### 2026-06-17 — second adaptive session (processed feedback/2026-06-16-sfvomu.json)
**Signal — and a sharp one.** Note: *"Many still feel easy but I think that's just because I'm
so familiar with c major scale. I think the harder keys will trip me up more."* The ratings
confirm his self-read exactly:
- Black keys HT worked — the jump didn't overwhelm: **B♭ 4, E♭ 4, G♭ 3** (G♭ the hardest).

- A minor HT **5**, A harmonic minor RH **5** — the raised 7th didn't faze him.
- The tell: **sevenths in C = 4** (genuinely challenging), but inversions HT, the arpeggio,

**What I changed and why.** His note is the thesis of the session: the C-based harmony is easy
because it's in C, so I moved it into harder keys — which doubles as black-key/sharp-key work.
- **Black keys nudged, not overhauled.** B♭/E♭ HT tempo 84→88 (they were 4s). G♭ (the 3) gets

- **Flat-scale ladder continues.** A♭ RH a second pass (slightly faster) to consolidate the 4;
  added **D♭ major RH** as the next flat key.
- **Minors advanced** — A harmonic minor RH→**HT**; added **D natural minor RH** for a new key

- **Chords leave C** (the explicit ask). Kept the C sevenths (the lone real 4), but moved the

- **Progressions harder two ways** — the G loop goes **hands-together**, and added a **fresh F

This is a bigger sweep than my usual 1–3 threads, but it's one coherent move — *transpose owned
material into harder keys* — not seven unrelated changes, and the spine (black-key scales, C
sevenths) carries over so progress stays legible. ~29 min, 14 exercises, validated clean.

**Watching for next:** does moving chords/progressions into G and F finally produce 3–4s
