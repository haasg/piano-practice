A rep is one clean trip through the whole loop, in time. Left hand drops the root, right hand blocks the triad on the beat. Slips don’t count — slow the tempo until the changes are clean.
What is this & the whole workout

Why progressions. Most pop, film and cover tunes are built from a handful of chord loops repeated. Learn to recognise and play the loop and a whole song collapses from hundreds of notes into “it’s that four-chord turn in this key.” That recognition is the single biggest lever on learning a piece faster.

How to play each one. Left hand plays the chord’s root as a single low note; right hand blocks the triad on the beat. Play the loop round and round until the changes are automatic. Roman numerals (I, V, vi, IV…) name each chord’s spot in the key — the same numerals, transposed, are why one shape unlocks the song in any key.
